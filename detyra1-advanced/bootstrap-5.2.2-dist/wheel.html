The Audi quattro GTO was a specialized version of the quattro intended for racing in the IMSA GT Championship. Entered into the 1989 IMSA GT Championship by Audi of America and driven by Hans-Joachim Stuck, the quattro GTO was essentially a standard Group B Audi quattro with a silhouette bodyshell and an all-wheel drive drivetrain. The car's 2.2L turbocharged flat-5 could produce 720 horsepower and was very successful, winning seven of thirteen races; despite this, Audi lost to Roush Racing.
